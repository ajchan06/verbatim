ALEX: Hey Marcus, thanks for making time. I know you're busy.

MARCUS: No worries, I had a cancellation actually. So you've got me for a bit.

ALEX: Perfect. So just to orient — I'm trying to understand the trial experience for Linkup, and you tried it recently and didn't continue. I'd love to understand why.

MARCUS: Yeah, I'll be honest with you. It came down to money in the end. Not entirely but mostly.

ALEX: Okay, let's start there then. Walk me through that thinking.

MARCUS: So I run a small creative agency, there's four of us. I was looking at the Team plan because we wanted everyone to have their own scheduling link but with shared availability, like a round-robin thing for new client calls. And I did the math and it was — what is it, twelve a head? So forty-eight bucks a month. Which when I say it out loud isn't crazy but for four people on a tool we use, you know, ten times a week, it felt like a lot.

ALEX: When you compare that to other tools you pay for, where does it sit?

MARCUS: It's not the most expensive thing we pay for. But it's a calendar. Like, my brain refuses to pay forty-eight bucks a month for a calendar. I pay less than that for Adobe. Well, I used to. Anyway. If it were six bucks a head I would've signed up without thinking about it.

ALEX: Got it. You mentioned that wasn't the only reason though.

MARCUS: Yeah. So setup was kind of a pain too. I think I sat down on a Tuesday night to get everyone set up and an hour later I was still in settings. There's a lot of screens before you get to the actual using it part. And then I had to do it again for each team member, basically walk them through. My designer Jess just gave up halfway through.

ALEX: When you say a lot of screens — do you remember roughly how many?

MARCUS: For the admin setup? Like a dozen feels right but I'm probably exaggerating. It was a lot of clicking. And some of it I get, like you have to connect your calendar, fine. But things like, do you want to send a reminder email, how long should it be, what's the subject line — I just want defaults. Pick good defaults. I'll change them later if I need to.

ALEX: That's helpful. The round-robin feature — did you actually get to try it?

MARCUS: I did, for like two days. And honestly it worked great, that part was the whole reason I was even considering it. When it worked, that was magic. New lead comes in, books a discovery call, it just lands on whoever has space. That's the dream. I'd pay for that. Just not forty-eight bucks for that.

ALEX: If pricing weren't an issue, would you have stayed?

MARCUS: Probably, yeah. If you put a gun to my head — I'd say there's like a sixty percent chance I would have stayed if it were half the price. The setup pain would've still annoyed me but I would have pushed through.

ALEX: What did you end up doing instead?

MARCUS: We're using Calendly's free tier for now, individual links only, no round robin. It's worse. But it's free.

ALEX: One last question — anything good about Linkup you'd want me to take back to the team?

MARCUS: Yeah, the actual booking page that clients see is really nice. Clean. My clients commented on it twice during the trial, which never happens. So the front end is good. The back end was where it lost me.

ALEX: Great, thank you Marcus. Really appreciate this.

MARCUS: Anytime. Tell them to cut the price.
