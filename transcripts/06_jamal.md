ALEX: Jamal, appreciate you fitting this in.

JAMAL: Of course. I've been wanting to give you all feedback for a while actually, so this is good timing.

ALEX: Okay great. Let's just start broad — how is Linkup working out for you?

JAMAL: For the most part, really well. The round-robin is the reason we bought it and that part is excellent. We route demo requests across like sixteen AEs and it just handles it. That's been a huge unlock. I have no notes on that part of the product.

ALEX: Anything else in the win column?

JAMAL: The team availability views are clean. The booking UX is fast. The metrics dashboard is okay — could be better but it's fine.

ALEX: Okay. Where do you have notes.

JAMAL: Salesforce. Look, the Salesforce integration is the thing that's making me nervous about renewal honestly. We need every booking to flow into Salesforce, attach to the right lead, fire the right workflows. And it works, like, sixty percent of the time. Maybe seventy. The other thirty percent we have bookings landing on contacts that don't exist or attaching to the wrong opportunity, or the activity log doesn't show up at all.

ALEX: Have you been working with our team on this?

JAMAL: Yeah, we've had I think four or five support tickets, two of them got escalated. The most recent answer was "we're rebuilding the integration." Which I appreciate the transparency but it's been a few months.

ALEX: Has this affected how you use the product?

JAMAL: We have a manual reconciliation process now where one of our ops people spends like four hours a week cleaning up Salesforce records that didn't sync correctly. So yeah, it has a cost. It's not a small one.

ALEX: When you say it's making you nervous about renewal — is that real or is that pressure?

JAMAL: It's real. Renewal is in November. If the Salesforce thing isn't solid by then I'm going to take a serious look at HubSpot's native scheduling or going back to Chili Piper. I don't want to. The round-robin is genuinely the best in market for us. But Salesforce is non-negotiable for our stack.

ALEX: Got it. That's important. Anything else?

JAMAL: Smaller stuff. Setting up new users is more clicks than it needs to be. Reporting could use cohort views. I'd love a Zapier action for "new booking created" with more fields. None of those are dealbreakers though, those are nice-to-haves.

ALEX: Last question — if you had to pick one thing to fix, what would it be?

JAMAL: Salesforce. Just Salesforce. Fix that and I'd extend the contract for two years tomorrow.

ALEX: Loud and clear. Thank you Jamal.

JAMAL: Thanks for asking.
