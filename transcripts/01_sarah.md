ALEX: Thanks for hopping on, Sarah. Just to set expectations, this is going to be pretty informal. I mostly want to hear about your experience signing up for Linkup and what made you decide not to continue. There are no wrong answers and you can skip anything you don't want to get into.

SARAH: Sure, yeah. Happy to. I don't have a ton of negative things to say honestly, it just didn't end up being for me.

ALEX: Got it. Maybe walk me back to the start — what made you sign up in the first place?

SARAH: Um, so I run a small private practice. Therapy. And I was using a paper calendar, basically, with email back and forth, and it was getting kind of ridiculous. A colleague mentioned Linkup, I think she heard about it on a podcast.

ALEX: Okay. And what did you do once you got into the product?

SARAH: I made an account and I think there was a — there was like a setup wizard? It asked me to connect my Google Calendar, which fine, that's expected. Then it wanted me to set my working hours, and then there was something about event types, and then meeting durations, and then a buffer time thing, and then I think it wanted me to pick a URL slug. By the time I got to the third or fourth screen I kind of just wanted to send a link to someone. That was the whole reason I was there.

ALEX: How many screens do you think it was, roughly?

SARAH: Honestly? Felt like seven. Maybe it was actually five. But it was a lot before I could do the thing.

ALEX: Did you finish the setup?

SARAH: I did, yeah. But I think the moment of, like, this isn't for me, was a few days later. So I had it set up, I sent a link to a new client, and she booked at a time that was — for context, I really need fifteen minutes between sessions. To write notes, to breathe, whatever. And Linkup booked her back-to-back with my existing client. And I just thought, how did that happen.

ALEX: So you had a buffer set, or you thought you had?

SARAH: I thought I had. I went back into the settings and the default — and I'm pretty sure it was the default — was zero. Zero buffer. Which is wild to me because who actually wants zero buffer between meetings.

ALEX: That's really useful, thank you. Did you adjust it?

SARAH: I did adjust it and then it worked fine. But by that point I'd had to email the client and reschedule, which is exactly the thing I was trying to avoid by getting a tool like this. And honestly the experience of that first booking going wrong kind of soured the whole thing for me. I figured if I missed that, what else am I going to miss.

ALEX: Mm, that makes sense. Were there other things that bothered you, or was that the main one?

SARAH: That was the main one really. I think if buffer time had defaulted to like ten minutes, or if the setup had asked me, even just asked me, hey what's your typical buffer, I would probably still be using it. It's a small thing but it cost me trust early.

ALEX: Did you look at any alternatives before cancelling?

SARAH: I went back to email honestly. I know that's the boring answer. I might try Calendly at some point but I'm a little gun-shy now.

ALEX: Last question — if a friend asked you about Linkup tomorrow, what would you tell them?

SARAH: I would say it's probably fine if you have time to configure it properly. But don't trust the defaults. That's what I'd tell them.

ALEX: Got it. Thank you, Sarah, this was super helpful.

SARAH: Yeah of course, good luck with it.
