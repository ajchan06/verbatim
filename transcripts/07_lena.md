ALEX: Hi Lena, thanks for the time.

LENA: Of course. I'm a fan of Linkup so I'm happy to help.

ALEX: That's nice to hear. Tell me how you use it.

LENA: I'm an executive coach, I have about thirty active clients, and basically all my coaching sessions and intro calls flow through Linkup. I have three event types — discovery call, regular session, and a longer planning session. Clients book themselves. It just works in the background.

ALEX: How long have you been with us?

LENA: Over a year. Maybe fourteen months? I came from Calendly because a friend showed me the booking page design, I just thought it was nicer.

ALEX: Is there anything you'd want us to do differently?

LENA: There's one big one for me. Notion. So all my client notes live in Notion. Each client has a page. When a session gets booked I have to manually go open Notion, find the right client page, add a session entry, link the calendar invite. If Linkup could just create or update a Notion page when a booking happens, with the client info filled in, that would save me real time. Like twenty minutes a week, which adds up.

ALEX: Got it. Is there a workaround you're using?

LENA: I have a Zapier zap that kind of does it but it's brittle, it only works for new clients, not returning ones. The matching logic is just too dumb. A native integration would actually understand "this booking is the same person as the Notion page named X."

ALEX: Anything else?

LENA: Honestly no. The product works for me as a solo person. I don't need most of the team features. The price is fair for what I get.

ALEX: Have you ever hit the mobile app?

LENA: Yeah, occasionally. It's not great but I almost never need it. My work happens at a desk.

ALEX: That's helpful context. Last thing — is there a way Linkup could make your business better that we haven't talked about?

LENA: Maybe better client-facing reminders? Like a more personal email template option, not just "Reminder: meeting at 3." I'd love to send something warmer. But that's small. The Notion thing is the real one.

ALEX: Got it. Thanks Lena.

LENA: Anytime.
