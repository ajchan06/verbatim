ALEX: Yuki, thank you for chatting. I know it's a little awkward to talk to a company you decided not to use.

YUKI: Ha, it's fine. I'm pretty direct so this should be easy.

ALEX: Perfect. Tell me what you ended up choosing.

YUKI: SavvyCal. I went with SavvyCal in the end. I had been on the Linkup trial for the full fourteen days and I had Calendly open in another tab and SavvyCal in another tab, basically running them in parallel.

ALEX: That's pretty thorough. What pushed you to SavvyCal?

YUKI: One feature mostly. The email embed thing. So in SavvyCal you can paste your availability into an email as actual clickable times. The recipient sees five suggested times, picks one, done. Linkup doesn't do that, it just sends a link. And I send a lot of emails to busy executives. Sending a busy executive a link to a third-party booking page is — half of them won't click. But suggested times directly in the email, they pick one in two seconds.

ALEX: That's a really specific use case.

YUKI: It's not that specific actually, anyone who works with executives runs into this. I bet if you talked to people who do consulting or sales or anything where the relationship matters, they'd say the same. It's a respect thing too — you're meeting people where they are, in email, instead of demanding they jump to your tool.

ALEX: That's useful framing. Were there other reasons you went with SavvyCal?

YUKI: Price was a factor. SavvyCal is a bit cheaper at my usage. And the mobile experience honestly, I tried the Linkup mobile app once and never opened it again. I do a lot from my phone. SavvyCal's mobile isn't great either to be fair, but it's slightly less bad.

ALEX: If Linkup had the email embed feature, would you have stayed?

YUKI: Probably yes. The email embed is the killer feature for me. The rest of Linkup is comparable, the booking page is nicer than SavvyCal's actually. But that one feature is decision-making for my workflow.

ALEX: Anything else?

YUKI: I'll say — your sales team was good. The person I talked to during my trial, Jordan I think, was helpful and didn't pressure me. So when I made my decision I almost felt bad. So a small mark in your favor on the human side.

ALEX: That's really nice to hear. Last question — if we built email embed and you knew about it, what would it take to switch you back?

YUKI: A free month and I'd test it again. I switch tools more than most people, I'm not loyal to SavvyCal. I'm loyal to whatever works best.

ALEX: Got it. Thank you Yuki, this is incredibly helpful.

YUKI: My pleasure. Tell your product team about email embed.
