ALEX: Hi Tom, thanks for being open to chatting.

TOM: Yeah no problem. Probably won't be that interesting honestly, I barely used it.

ALEX: That's actually exactly what I want to hear about — the people who didn't get far in. So you signed up and then what?

TOM: So I'm a freelance copywriter, I work for myself, I get maybe three or four new prospect calls a month. My current setup is, somebody emails me, we go back and forth on times, eventually we land on one. It's annoying but it works.

ALEX: What pushed you to try Linkup?

TOM: I saw a tweet thread about it. Someone was talking about how their booking conversion went up after they switched to it. I thought, fine, I'll try it.

ALEX: And what happened when you got into the product?

TOM: So I get in and there's a setup flow. And I'm trying to remember the steps — connect your calendar, set your hours, pick a URL, set up your first event type, choose durations, set up notifications, branding stuff, I think there was a payments thing too — and I'm like, hold on. I just want a link. I just want a button I can press that gives me a link I can send to someone. Why is this so much work upfront.

ALEX: How far did you get?

TOM: I gave up at the event type screen I think. Just closed the tab. I came back to it like two days later and finished but the feeling was already there of, this is too much for what I need.

ALEX: When you say "too much for what I need" — can you say more about that?

TOM: Yeah I mean, I'm one person. I take meetings on Tuesdays and Thursdays. I don't need event types and routing and notifications and branding. I need one link. I think the tool is built for teams and it's making solo users do team-level setup.

ALEX: Did you keep using it after you finished setup?

TOM: I sent maybe two links. And then the trial expired and I just didn't renew. Honestly forgot about it until you emailed me.

ALEX: If there was, like, a "I'm a freelancer, just give me a link" mode — would that have helped?

TOM: Yeah, probably. Like a five-second setup. Pick your hours, go. I'd actually pay for that, like five bucks a month or whatever.

ALEX: Helpful. Thanks Tom.

TOM: Sure thing.
