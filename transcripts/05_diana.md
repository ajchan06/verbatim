ALEX: Diana, thanks for making the time. I know you've been a customer for a while now.

DIANA: Yeah, almost a year I think? Time flies.

ALEX: I'm trying to understand what's working for our most engaged users and where the rough edges still are. So I'll ask you some things you love and some things you wish were different.

DIANA: Okay, I have opinions on both. Fire away.

ALEX: Let's start with the love. What's the feature that, if it went away tomorrow, you would actually be upset?

DIANA: Round-robin routing. Hundred percent. That is the thing. Our SDR team uses it for every inbound demo request and it basically distributes load across the team automatically based on who's available. Before Linkup we had a Slack channel where SDRs would call dibs on leads and it was chaos. Now it just works.

ALEX: That's great to hear. Anything specific about how it's implemented that you appreciate?

DIANA: The fact that I can weight it. So newer SDRs get fewer leads while they're ramping, and senior people pick up the slack. That's actually unusual, not every tool lets you do that. And the round-robin respects everyone's calendars in real time, so if Jess is in a meeting until 4 her slot doesn't get offered. Sounds basic but you'd be surprised.

ALEX: Anything else in the "love" column?

DIANA: The booking page itself is nice. It looks professional. We've gotten compliments from prospects, which never used to happen with Calendly.

ALEX: Now the other side. Where does it fall short for you?

DIANA: Honestly? Mobile. The mobile app is rough. I'm a head of ops, I'm on calls all day, I check things from my phone constantly, and the mobile experience is like a totally different product. It's slower, missing features, the navigation is weird. I just don't use it on mobile, I wait until I'm at a laptop. Which is fine for me but a lot of my team complains about it too.

ALEX: What features specifically are missing on mobile?

DIANA: Routing rules I can't edit on mobile. Team availability I can't see properly. Reports are basically web-only. The basics like sending a link or checking my own calendar are fine but anything administrative I'm doing on web.

ALEX: Anything else on the wish-list side?

DIANA: Notion integration. Please. Half my team lives in Notion, we have meeting notes in Notion, we have OKRs in Notion. If a meeting booked through Linkup could auto-create a Notion page with the attendee info and a template, that would save us so much manual work. I know you have a Google Docs integration but it's not where we are.

ALEX: How important is that on a scale of like, nice to have versus would actually shift behavior?

DIANA: It wouldn't make me churn if you didn't build it. But if you built it I would tell everyone I know.

ALEX: Got it. Is there anything else that's frustrated you over the past year? Outages, bugs, support issues?

DIANA: Support has been great honestly. There was a weird thing with timezone display about six months ago where it would show times in UTC for some users — that was annoying but you fixed it. No major outages I can remember.

ALEX: Beautiful. Last question — how would you describe Linkup to a peer who hadn't heard of it?

DIANA: I'd say it's Calendly but actually built for teams. The round-robin alone makes it worth switching for any sales org. Just don't expect much from the mobile app.

ALEX: Diana, this was incredibly helpful. Thank you.

DIANA: Anytime.
