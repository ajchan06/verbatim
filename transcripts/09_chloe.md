ALEX: Chloe, thanks for getting on. I know you all evaluated us recently and didn't move forward.

CHLOE: Yeah, we did a two-week team trial. I'd say we got pretty deep into it. We had a real conversation about whether to buy.

ALEX: What was the team you were evaluating it for?

CHLOE: My team. Customer support. We're thirty-five people, all customer-facing. We do scheduled callbacks for premium customers and we wanted a tool that let customers self-serve scheduling instead of going through our queue.

ALEX: Got it. Walk me through how the eval went.

CHLOE: I'd say the trial actually went pretty well functionally. The product does what it says. We had four CSAs on it for two weeks, they got the hang of it quickly, customers were able to book without issues. The booking experience was solid.

ALEX: So where did it fall apart?

CHLOE: Couple of places. The biggest was honestly pricing. Thirty-five people at twelve a month is over four hundred dollars a month, like five thousand a year. For a scheduling tool. My CFO looked at that and said find a cheaper option. I tried to make the case but I didn't have great ammunition.

ALEX: When you say cheaper option — what's the comparison?

CHLOE: We were also looking at HubSpot's native scheduling because we already pay for HubSpot. It's worse, like noticeably worse, but it's free for us. So the question was, is Linkup five thousand dollars a year better than free. And the answer my CFO came to was no.

ALEX: What would have made the math work?

CHLOE: Volume discount above twenty-five seats would have done it. Or if there was a customer-support-tier pricing, like we don't need all the sales-routing features, we just need basic scheduling for thirty-five people. If you'd offered me that at six a head I'd have signed.

ALEX: You said couple of places — what was the other one?

CHLOE: The Salesforce integration. We use Salesforce for our CRM, well it's actually Salesforce Service Cloud. And the integration we tested had a bunch of weird behavior. Bookings sometimes didn't show up in the case record. Activity attribution was wrong. My team would have lost trust in it pretty quickly.

ALEX: How big a factor was that compared to pricing?

CHLOE: If pricing had been okay, the Salesforce stuff would have been the dealbreaker by itself. So they're kind of independent issues. Both pretty serious.

ALEX: That's helpful. If we came back to you in six months with fixed integration and a better volume price — would you re-engage?

CHLOE: Honestly yes. I liked the product. My team liked it. I'd love to revisit.

ALEX: Thank you Chloe, this is really useful.

CHLOE: Sure thing. Good luck.
